About me

I am Ashwani Kumar, a computational engineering researcher specializing in multiphysics and multiscale modeling. Currently pursuing my Doctor of Science in Mechanical Engineering at ETH Zürich, I focus on advanced simulation techniques for manufacturing processes such as Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS). My work combines finite element methods, machine learning, and material modeling to bridge microscale phenomena with macroscale engineering applications.

With professional experience spanning academia and industry—from developing fracture models in complex materials to leading structural analysis in the automotive sector—I bring both technical depth and practical problem-solving skills. I am passionate about applying computational tools to innovate in manufacturing, materials science, and product design.

Outside of work, I enjoy exploring new technologies and collaborating on multidisciplinary projects that push the boundaries of engineering research.
